CPP, that’s part of it, but there are other factors at play. <\/p>\n

PLoS Biology and Medicine are the only journals that have professional editors handling manuscripts. The community journals (Genetics, Pathogens, CompBio & NTD) use academic editors, as does PLoS ONE. However the community journals have a greater staff\/published paper ratio, in part because they are smaller and don’t have PLoS ONE’s efficiencies of scale, but also because they provide more staff support for the AEs. This contributes to the differences in price. <\/p>\n

But it’s also important to note that, because it costs money to oversee peer review and provide editing (mostly in labor spent on identifying and chasing editors and reviewers), and only accepted papers generate revenue, a larger fraction of the costs of review\/editing has to be borne by each paper at PLoS Biology, Medicine and the community journals compared to PLoS ONE. In principle it would make more sense to charge for review, but there are sociological reasons that make this difficult to do now.<\/p>\n

Our long-term goal is to eliminate as many of these costs as possible. Much of the cost of publishing is labor, and much of this labor could be eliminated with better technology, including better authoring tools (which would eliminate costs associated with converting submitted files into publication ready XML & PDFs), better methods for matching editors and reviewers to papers etc…<\/p>\n"},"link":"https:\/\/www.michaeleisen.org\/blog\/?p=985&cpage=1#comment-58233","status":"approved","type":"comment","author_avatar_urls":{"24":"https:\/\/secure.gravatar.com\/avatar\/59aabfdb269ff01455d71541125a74d0?s=24&d=mm&r=g","48":"https:\/\/secure.gravatar.com\/avatar\/59aabfdb269ff01455d71541125a74d0?s=48&d=mm&r=g","96":"https:\/\/secure.gravatar.com\/avatar\/59aabfdb269ff01455d71541125a74d0?s=96&d=mm&r=g"},"meta":[],"_links":{"self":[{"href":"https:\/\/www.michaeleisen.org\/blog\/index.php?rest_route=\/wp\/v2\/comments\/58233"}],"collection":[{"href":"https:\/\/www.michaeleisen.org\/blog\/index.php?rest_route=\/wp\/v2\/comments"}],"author":[{"embeddable":true,"href":"https:\/\/www.michaeleisen.org\/blog\/index.php?rest_route=\/wp\/v2\/users\/1"}],"up":[{"embeddable":true,"post_type":"post","href":"https:\/\/www.michaeleisen.org\/blog\/index.php?rest_route=\/wp\/v2\/posts\/985"}],"in-reply-to":[{"embeddable":true,"href":"https:\/\/www.michaeleisen.org\/blog\/index.php?rest_route=\/wp\/v2\/comments\/58211"}]}},{"id":58211,"post":985,"parent":0,"author":0,"author_name":"Comrade PhysioProf","author_url":"http:\/\/freethoughtblogs.com\/physioprof","date":"2012-02-21T18:42:17","date_gmt":"2012-02-22T01:42:17","content":{"rendered":"

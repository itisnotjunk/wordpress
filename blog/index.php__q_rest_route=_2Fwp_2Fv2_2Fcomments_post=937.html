Elsevier, as we know it now, is NOT the five-generation publishers (from 1580 to 1712) of the 16th to the 18th century. Please stop contributing to disseminate this false information that we hear too often. In fact, Elsevier Publishing Company was established in 1880 by the Rotterdam bookseller, Jacobus George Robbers who had absolutely no relation with the Elsevier. There is no historical or legal continuity between the House of Elsevier and the later. Incidentally, it is important to note that the “new Elsevier” simply usurped the “real Elsevier” printer’s mark (the man and the oliver tree). To know more, read A.J.

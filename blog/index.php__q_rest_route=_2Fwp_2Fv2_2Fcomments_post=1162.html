After reading this article and your previous article on the subject, plus the linked articles, a thought struck me:
\nWhile the pace of formal peer review is clearly slow and painful, at the same time a large quantity of informal peer review is being lost.
\nHow often have you read a paper and thought to yourself: “If I were a reviewer for this paper, I would have pointed out ____.”
\nHow often have journal club speakers described their own critique of a paper, or compared two interesting papers to each other?
\nRight now there is no way to capture that information.
\nPerhaps a journal could proceed as follows:
\nAuthors submit a paper, which is initially flagged as needing peer review.
\nThe paper is posted publicly and journal members are welcome to provide feedback.
\nAfter some time, the authors post an updated version; the original version and reviews remain available.
\nEventually the editor or moderator can set the paper’s flag from “under review” to “reviewed”, but this has no impact on its availability.
\nJournal members retain the ability to comment on papers after the flag is changed, and authors can reply to comments on their own papers.
\nJournal members should have the ability to upvote both papers and review comments.
\nIn terms of the business model, open-access charges could be levied twice, once at initial posting, and once when the paper changes from “under review” to “reviewed”
\nAs others have suggested, there should be points awarded to reviewers, with extra points for the first few reviews on a paper and extra points for highly-rated reviews and authors of highly-rated papers.
\nThese points could be exchanged for lower open-access fees levied on the author’s next paper, because they are contributing value to the journal.
\nIdeally, the journal should suggest newly posted papers for review each time a member logs into the journal website.
\nJournal registration should require a full name, but a pseudonym should be optional to label review comments. Graduate students and postdocs should be allowed to join and become reviewers.
